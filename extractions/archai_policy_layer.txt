ARCHAI POLICY LAYER MODULE

PURPOSE:
Defines global rules, constraints, and decision policies for the entire ARCHAI system. Governs allowed behaviors, forbidden transitions, and arbitration between competing modules.

COMPONENTS:
- Policy Rule Engine
- Constraint Evaluator
- Arbitration Logic
- Policy Memory Map
- Policy Enforcement Hooks

POLICY TYPES:
- HARD POLICIES:
  - Cannot be overridden.
  - Enforced by Safety Shell.
  - Examples: invariant protection, forbidden transitions, drift monotonicity.

- SOFT POLICIES:
  - Can be adjusted by governance.
  - Examples: exploration depth, refinement cycles, timing preferences.

- CONTEXTUAL POLICIES:
  - Activated based on domain, risk level, or system state.
  - Examples: high-risk mode, low-risk mode, experimental mode.

RULE ENGINE:
- Evaluates policies on every kernel cycle.
- Applies constraints before TCE transitions.
- Blocks unsafe or forbidden actions.
- Emits policy events for governance.

ARBITRATION LOGIC:
- Resolves conflicts between modules.
- Prioritizes safety > invariants > governance > TCE.
- Uses weighted decision model for soft conflicts.

POLICY MEMORY MAP:
- Stores persistent policy definitions.
- Tracks policy history and overrides.
- Maintains contextual activation states.

EVENTS:
- onPolicyViolation(rule)
- onPolicyOverride(rule)
- onPolicyActivate(context)
- onPolicyDeactivate(context)

API:
- policy.set(rule, value)
- policy.get(rule)
- policy.enable(context)
- policy.disable(context)
- policy.check(rule)
- policy.getStatus()

ARCHAI USE:
Provides global decision governance, ensuring all modules operate within defined behavioral boundaries and system-wide rules.
