TCE-4: Lifecycle Specification

PURPOSE:
Defines the full lifecycle of a TCE-driven decision process.

LIFECYCLE PHASES:
PHASE 1 — CHAOTIC EXPLORATION
  - High variability
  - Wide search radius
  - Driven by initial Ψ(t)

PHASE 2 — COLLAPSE (INVERSE PERIOD-DOUBLING)
  - Structure emerges
  - Chaos dies
  - Ψ(t) crosses bifurcation thresholds

PHASE 3 — PERIODIC REFINEMENT
  - Pattern stabilization
  - Iterative improvement
  - Reduced variability

PHASE 4 — CONVERGENT FINALIZATION
  - Commitment
  - Output crystallization
  - Drift approaches stable region

PHASE 5 — OPTIONAL REHEAT
  - Ψ(t) reversed
  - Re-entry into chaotic regime
  - Used when stuck or suboptimal

ARCHAI USE:
- Full decision lifecycle.
- Autonomous exploration and convergence.
