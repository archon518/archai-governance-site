SYSTEM MAP: MECHANISM INVARIANT

1. INVARIANT
A mechanism is the deterministic transformation engine of a system: an organized pattern of entities and activities that reliably produces a phenomenon through constrained, rule‑bound interaction.

2. STRUCTURAL COMPONENTS
- Entities — the structural units
- Activities — the operations performed
- Organization — the relational ordering
- Phenomenon — the produced output

3. MECHANISM TYPES
- Complex‑systems mechanisms — stable, repeatable, hierarchical
- Mechanistic processes — spatio‑temporal propagation
- Hybrid mechanisms — combined structural + processual

4. FUNCTIONAL ROLES
- Explain — reveal how the phenomenon is produced
- Predict — anticipate outcomes under new conditions
- Justify — support causal claims and decisions

5. CONSTRAINT RULES
A mechanism is valid only if:
- its entities are bounded
- its activities are deterministic
- its organization is coherent
- its output is reproducible

6. DRIFT MAPPING
Mechanism drift occurs when:
- entities degrade
- activities distort
- organization collapses

7. FAILURE MAPPING
A mechanism fails when:
- it cannot produce its phenomenon
- its constraints are violated
- its organization becomes incoherent

8. ADAPTATION MAPPING
Mechanisms adapt through:
- reconfiguration
- compensation
- replacement

UNIFIED ARCHONOMY MECHANISM NODE
Mechanism = the invariant, constrained, organized transformation engine that produces system phenomena, governs flows, shapes drift, determines failure, and enables adaptation.
