GENERATIVE MECHANISM: MECHANISMS

CORE GENERATOR
Mechanisms arise when entities capable of activity become organized under constraints that force deterministic transformation patterns.

GENERATIVE STEPS
1. Entity Formation — structural units emerge or are defined.
2. Activity Assignment — operations become possible between entities.
3. Constraint Imposition — boundaries restrict possible interactions.
4. Organizational Binding — relations lock into a stable pattern.
5. Phenomenon Emergence — the mechanism produces a reproducible output.

GENERATIVE PROPERTIES
- Self‑limiting: constraints prevent chaotic behavior.
- Self‑stabilizing: organization maintains coherence.
- Self‑propagating: outputs can feed into higher‑order mechanisms.

META‑INVARIANT
A mechanism is generated when constraint + organization + activity converge into a stable transformation engine.

ARCHONOMY POSITION
This is the root generator for all mechanism nodes in the system.
