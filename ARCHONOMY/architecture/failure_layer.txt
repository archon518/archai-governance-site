FAILURE LAYER

DEFINITION
Failure is the breakdown of a mechanism, entity, or activity such that it can no longer sustain valid flow, maintain constraints, or preserve structural integrity.

FAILURE PRINCIPLES
1. Failure emerges when drift exceeds system tolerance.
2. Failure propagates unless isolated by containment mechanisms.
3. Failure reveals the limits of system organization and constraint design.
4. Failure is not instantaneous; it is the culmination of accumulated distortions.

TYPES OF FAILURE
- Mechanism Failure — structural collapse or invalidation of organizational pathways.
- Constraint Failure — limits no longer regulate flow or activity.
- Activity Failure — processes desynchronize, distort, or halt.
- Entity Failure — components degrade beyond functional thresholds.

FAILURE TRIGGERS
- overload of flow,
- constraint violation,
- accumulated drift,
- environmental shock,
- internal instability.

UNIFIED FAILURE RULE
Failure is the irreversible point where system mechanisms can no longer maintain coherent behavior.
