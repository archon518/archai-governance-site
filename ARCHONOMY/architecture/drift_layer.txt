DRIFT LAYER

DEFINITION
Drift is the gradual, cumulative deviation of system mechanisms, entities, or activities away from their intended structure, constraints, or operational behavior.

DRIFT PRINCIPLES
1. Drift accumulates over time unless corrected.
2. Drift emerges from micro‑variations in mechanisms, entities, or activities.
3. Drift is detectable through divergence from expected flow patterns.
4. Drift is reversible only when structural integrity remains intact.

TYPES OF DRIFT
- Structural Drift — slow deformation of mechanism organization.
- Constraint Drift — weakening or shifting of operational limits.
- Activity Drift — misalignment in timing, magnitude, or coordination.
- Entity Drift — degradation or mutation of system components.

DRIFT TRIGGERS
- repeated micro-failures,
- environmental pressure,
- entropy accumulation,
- uncorrected flow distortions.

UNIFIED DRIFT RULE
Drift is the system’s natural tendency toward disorder unless stabilized by corrective mechanisms.
