ADAPTATION LAYER

DEFINITION
Adaptation is the system’s capacity to modify mechanisms, constraints, entities, or activities in response to drift, failure, or environmental change while preserving overall coherence.

ADAPTATION PRINCIPLES
1. Adaptation restores stability by counteracting drift.
2. Adaptation restructures mechanisms without collapsing organization.
3. Adaptation modifies constraints to re‑establish valid flow.
4. Adaptation is successful only when it preserves system identity.

TYPES OF ADAPTATION
- Mechanism Adaptation — reorganization of structural pathways.
- Constraint Adaptation — recalibration of operational limits.
- Activity Adaptation — retiming, rescaling, or re‑coordinating processes.
- Entity Adaptation — upgrading, repairing, or replacing components.

ADAPTATION TRIGGERS
- accumulated drift approaching failure thresholds,
- repeated flow distortions,
- environmental pressure requiring reconfiguration,
- internal instability demanding correction.

UNIFIED ADAPTATION RULE
Adaptation is the system’s corrective restructuring process that restores functional coherence without losing identity.
