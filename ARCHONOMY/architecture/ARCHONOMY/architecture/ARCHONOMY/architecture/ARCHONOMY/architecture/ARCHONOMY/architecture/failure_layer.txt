FAILURE LAYER (OVERWRITTEN)

DEFINITION
Failure occurs when a mechanism can no longer produce its phenomenon under its governing constraints.

MECHANISM-ALIGNED FAILURE PRINCIPLES
1. Failure results from loss of determinacy.
2. Failure occurs when organization collapses.
3. Failure emerges when constraints are violated.
4. Failure is confirmed when output becomes non-reproducible.

FAILURE TYPES
- Entity Failure — structural collapse.
- Activity Failure — operational breakdown.
- Organizational Failure — relational incoherence.
- Phenomenal Failure — output invalidity.

FAILURE CONDITIONS
A mechanism is failed when:
- it cannot transform input to output,
- it produces inconsistent phenomena,
- it violates its governing law.

UNIFIED FAILURE RULE
Failure is the collapse of mechanistic capability.
