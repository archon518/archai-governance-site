DRIFT LAYER (OVERWRITTEN)

DEFINITION
Drift is the deviation of a mechanism from its invariant transformation pattern.

MECHANISM-ALIGNED DRIFT PRINCIPLES
1. Drift originates from entity degradation.
2. Drift emerges from activity distortion.
3. Drift increases when organizational coherence weakens.
4. Drift accumulates when constraints loosen or fail.

DRIFT TYPES
- Structural Drift — entity-level degradation.
- Operational Drift — activity-level distortion.
- Organizational Drift — relational breakdown.
- Phenomenal Drift — inconsistent outputs.

DRIFT CONSEQUENCES
- Reduced reproducibility.
- Increased failure probability.
- Loss of system stability.

UNIFIED DRIFT RULE
Drift is the erosion of mechanistic determinacy.
