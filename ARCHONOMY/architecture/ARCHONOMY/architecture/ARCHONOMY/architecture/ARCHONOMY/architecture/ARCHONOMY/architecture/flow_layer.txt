FLOW LAYER (OVERWRITTEN)

DEFINITION
Flow is the propagation of features, states, or signals through system mechanisms along deterministic, constrained pathways.

MECHANISM-ALIGNED FLOW PRINCIPLES
1. Flow occurs only through valid mechanisms.
2. Flow direction is determined by organizational ordering.
3. Flow magnitude is limited by entity and activity constraints.
4. Flow reproducibility depends on mechanism stability.

FLOW TYPES
- Mechanistic Process Flow — spatio-temporal propagation.
- Complex-System Flow — multi-entity coordinated transformation.
- Hybrid Flow — combined structural + processual propagation.

FLOW FAILURE CONDITIONS
Flow breaks when:
- mechanism organization collapses,
- constraints are violated,
- activities distort,
- entities degrade.

UNIFIED FLOW RULE
Flow is the expression of mechanism behavior across time and structure.
