ADAPTATION LAYER (OVERWRITTEN)

DEFINITION
Adaptation is the modification of a mechanism to preserve system identity under changing conditions.

MECHANISM-ALIGNED ADAPTATION PRINCIPLES
1. Adaptation reorganizes mechanisms without breaking identity.
2. Adaptation compensates for drift and partial failure.
3. Adaptation modifies constraints to restore determinacy.
4. Adaptation stabilizes flow under new conditions.

ADAPTATION TYPES
- Reconfiguration — structural rearrangement.
- Compensation — activity-level substitution.
- Replacement — entity or mechanism substitution.
- Reinforcement — strengthening constraints.

ADAPTATION CONDITIONS
Adaptation is valid when:
- identity is preserved,
- determinacy is restored,
- organization remains coherent.

UNIFIED ADAPTATION RULE
Adaptation is the restoration of mechanistic viability.
