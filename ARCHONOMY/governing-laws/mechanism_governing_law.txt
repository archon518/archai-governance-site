GOVERNING LAW: MECHANISMS

LAW STATEMENT
A mechanism must transform inputs into outputs through a deterministic, constrained, and organized interaction pattern that preserves system identity and produces a reproducible phenomenon.

LAW CONDITIONS
1. Structural Determinacy — entities must be bounded and identifiable.
2. Activity Determinacy — operations must follow invariant rules.
3. Organizational Coherence — relations must maintain causal order.
4. Phenomenal Reproducibility — outputs must be consistent under identical conditions.

LAW CONSEQUENCES
- If determinacy fails → drift.
- If coherence fails → failure.
- If reproducibility fails → invalid mechanism.

LAW DOMAIN
Applies to all system layers: structural, functional, flow, drift, failure, adaptation.

UNIFIED FORM
A mechanism is lawful only when its transformation pattern remains invariant under its constraints.
